# HR-Analytics-Dashboard
## HR Analytics Dashboard 📊
Project Overview

The HR Analytics Dashboard is a data visualization project designed to analyze employee attrition and workforce trends within an organization. The dashboard provides key insights into employee demographics, attrition patterns, salary distribution, and job roles to help HR teams make data-driven decisions.

The goal of this project is to identify the factors contributing to employee attrition and help organizations improve employee retention strategies.

## Key Metrics Displayed

The dashboard highlights several important HR metrics:

Total Employees: 1470

Total Attrition: 237 employees

Attrition Rate: 16.1%

Average Age: 37 years

Average Salary: 6.5K

Average Years at Company: 7 years

These KPIs provide a quick overview of workforce composition and attrition levels.

## Dashboard Insights
1. Attrition by Education

This visualization shows how employee attrition varies across different educational backgrounds.

Life Sciences: 38% (highest attrition)

Medical: 27%

Marketing: 15%

Technical Degree: 14%

This insight helps HR understand which educational backgrounds experience higher turnover.

2. Attrition by Age

The age group 26–35 years shows the highest attrition (116 employees).

Other age groups include:

18–25: 44 employees

36–45: 43 employees

46–55: 26 employees

55+: 8 employees

This suggests that mid-career employees are more likely to leave the organization.

3. Attrition by Salary Slab

Attrition is highest among employees with lower salary ranges.

Up to 5K: 163 employees

5K–10K: 49 employees

10K–15K: 20 employees

15K+: 5 employees

This indicates that lower salary levels may contribute significantly to employee turnover.

4. Attrition by Years at Company

Employees with 1–2 years of experience show the highest attrition.
Attrition gradually decreases as employees spend more years with the company.

This insight suggests early-stage employees may require better onboarding, engagement, and career growth opportunities.

5. Attrition by Job Role

The roles with the highest attrition include:

Laboratory Technician: 62

Sales Executive: 57

Research Scientist: 47

Sales Representative: 33

These roles may require further analysis to understand workload, compensation, or job satisfaction issues.

6. Gender-based Attrition

The dashboard also compares attrition by gender:

Male: 143

Female: 80

This helps HR understand gender distribution in employee turnover.
